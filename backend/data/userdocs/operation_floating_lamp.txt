OPERATION FLOATING LAMP
MISSION OVERVIEW & STATUS REPORT

Classification: SECRET — NOFORN. Authorised recipients only. Do not reproduce or transmit without authorisation from the Maritime Intelligence Unit.

1. Background
Operation Floating Lamp is a covert signals intelligence (SIGINT) collection programme employing a network of autonomous oceanographic buoys modified to carry low-observable passive RF intercept payloads. The buoys are indistinguishable from standard oceanographic monitoring equipment operated by civilian research institutions, providing persistent deniable coverage of priority maritime corridors.

The programme was authorised under Maritime Standing Order 22-F in September 2025 following a gap assessment that identified a 140-nautical-mile blind corridor in the northern passage where adversary submarine traffic had been detected but could not be reliably attributed.

4. Operational Security
Buoy cover identities are registered under the fictitious civilian programme designation "North Atlantic Oceanographic Survey — Phase III" administered through a front organisation. Any enquiry from civilian maritime authorities should be routed immediately to the MIU Operations Desk (secure line: ext. 7741). Do not confirm or deny programme existence to any non-cleared party.

Recovery of any LAMP buoy by non-authorised parties triggers automatic payload sanitisation via the onboard destruct controller. Destruct confirmation is logged to the MIU operations server within 15 minutes of activation.

5. Fault Report — LAMP-03
On 2026-04-17, LAMP-03 burst transmissions indicated degraded RF intercept sensitivity (noise floor +8 dB above baseline). Battery telemetry nominal. Assessment: probable seawater ingress into the RF module housing, consistent with storm conditions recorded in the deployment area on 2026-04-14 to 2026-04-15 (sea state 6). LAMP-03 is collecting at reduced sensitivity pending a recovery and maintenance sortie currently scheduled for 2026-05-10.
