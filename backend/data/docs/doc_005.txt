ORBITOPS Q1 2026 SCIENCE HIGHLIGHTS

Classification: UNCLASSIFIED. Approved for unrestricted public release.
Contact: communications@orbitops.example.com

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE — 10 April 2026

OrbitOps is proud to announce the science highlights from the first quarter of 2026, showcasing the outstanding research enabled by our orbital platforms.

Earth Observation Milestone
Our multispectral imaging constellation completed its 100,000th cloud-free observation of agricultural land cover across Southeast Asia, contributing directly to food-security modelling conducted by partner universities. Over 240 research groups worldwide have accessed this open data set since its release in January.

Atmospheric Science Results
Instruments aboard Platform ECHO-2 recorded an unexpected stratospheric aerosol injection event in late February, likely associated with the 2026-02-19 Tambora II eruption. The high-resolution vertical profiles captured will be shared with the World Meteorological Organisation to refine climate impact forecasts.

Education Partnership
The OrbitOps Schools Programme engaged 14,200 students across 38 countries during Q1, with live mission communication sessions, data analysis workshops, and a student-designed experiment now manifested for Q3 launch.

Upcoming Events
- Open Data Day webinar: 2026-04-25, 14:00 UTC. Register at orbitops.example.com/events.
- Annual Science Symposium: 2026-06-10 to 2026-06-12, Geneva.

OrbitOps remains committed to open science, international collaboration, and inspiring the next generation of space professionals.
